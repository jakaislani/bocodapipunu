Princess Leonor Parents: Meet Queen Letizia of Spain And Felipe VI of Spain

Princess Leonor parents-The heir presumptive to the Spanish throne, Leonor, Princess of Asturias was born on October 31, 2005, in Madrid, Spain.

Who are Princess Leonor’s parents?

Princess Leonor was born to Queen Letizia of Spain and Felipe VI of Spain. She has a sister named Infanta Sofía of Spain.

Who is Queen Letizia of Spain?

Queen Letizia of Spain, real name Letizia Ortiz Rocasolano is Queen of Spain as the wife of King Felipe VI. Letizia came from a middle-class family. She was born on September 15, 1972, and she is currently 51 years of age.

Prior to joining CNN+ and Televisión Española as a news anchor, she was a journalist for ABC and EFE. She wed Alonso Guerrero Pérez in 1998, but the two parted ways the next year.

Who is Felipe VI of Spain?

Felipe VI of Spain is the King of Spain. As per the Spanish Constitution, the monarch has the position of supreme representative of Spain in foreign affairs, in addition to being the head of state and commander-in-chief of the Spanish Armed Forces, holding the military rank of Captain General.

Princess Leonor life

Leonor visited the San Javier Air Force Base in Murcia for the first time on official business in May 2014. After King Juan Carlos signed the Abdication Act on June 18, 2014, Leonor’s father, King Felipe VI, took the throne on June 19, 2014, at midnight, and Leonor became the presumed heir and Princess of Asturias.

At the Museo de Cera in Madrid, a wax replica of Leonor was unveiled in October 2014. Leonor took her First Communion on May 20, 2015, in accordance with Catholic tradition.

In accordance with the 1975 Spanish constitution, Leonor, the eldest of Felipe’s two daughters, is the first in line to succeed in the Spanish monarchy under a system of male-preference cognatic primogeniture.

However, Leonor would fall out of the line of succession and once more become an infanta of Spain if her father had a legitimate son while still king.

The birth of Leonor and her younger sister Sofía put a stop to the talks of altering the succession law to absolute primogeniture, which would have allowed the eldest child to inherit regardless of gender.

In 2009, absolute primogeniture replaced male preference for Spanish titles of nobility; nevertheless, as of 2023, no laws pertaining to the succession to the throne have been approved.

Her father gave her the Order of the Golden Fleece the day before her tenth birthday. The Council of Ministers also gave its approval to the design of her guidon and personal standard.

In a formal ceremony held at the Royal Palace of Madrid in January 2018, the King bestowed Leonor the Golden Fleece collar in honor of King Felipe’s fiftieth birthday.

In September 2018, Leonor went to Covadonga with her parents to commemorate the 1,300th anniversary of the Kingdom of Asturias, marking her first public appearance outside of the palace.

At her first public address, which took place on October 31, 2018, Leonor recited the first article of the Spanish Constitution at the Instituto Cervantes in Madrid.

She gave her debut speech in Spanish, Catalan, English, and Arabic on November 4, 2019, during the Princess of Girona Foundation awards in Barcelona.

On March 24, 2021, she participated in her first solo public event by going to a celebration honoring the Instituto Cervantes’ 30th anniversary.

She took her first official international travel on July 16, 2022. With the help of her younger sister, Infanta Sofía, she accomplished the task without her parents’ presence.

They went to a Spain vs. Denmark game at the UEFA Women’s Euro 2022 together. When Leonor visited the Spanish Red Cross headquarters in Madrid in December 2022, she got to know young volunteers from The Red Cross Youth, the organization’s youth division.

Leonor pledged allegiance to the Spanish Flag on October 7, 2023, at the General Military Academy of Zaragoza.

As mandated by the Spanish Constitution, the Princess took an oath in front of the Spanish Parliament on October 31, 2023, the day she turned eighteen. At the Royal Palace in Madrid, King Felipe bestowed upon her the title of Knight Grand Cross with Collar of the Order of Charles III after she had taken her oath.
